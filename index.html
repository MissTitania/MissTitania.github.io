I'm Titania Emmons, a New York City programmer who studied computer science at Caltech. My primary interest is web development, but formerly I've focused on both AI/machine learning and information theory. You can browse this site to check out some of my more recent coding projects!

I have a strong mathematics background and previously taught the subject at a public high school. Mostly I handled precalculus, with a smattering of other courses. I think it pays greatly to see information organized so that ideas and motivations follow naturally from each other, so it was very gratifying to be able to structure things that way to make new information feel intuitive. It's made me into a great verbal communicator, and who doesn't get use out of that!?!

For fun I play games of all varieties, be it board, strategy, card, video, tabletop, or sports. To me the essential divide between games and life is that games tell in objective terms how to win, and I find this an invigorating shift. Many of these I undertake competitively. Most notable is bridge; I was on the US Bridge Federation international team in 2011 and have previously played for hire. Currently I also compete in Pokemon tournaments, and in the past I've competed at volleyball, fencing, Magic: the Gathering, been named the world champion at numerous board games published by the Sirlin Games company, and a number of other competitive gaming endeavors.

Black:White:

The server of Pokemon Showdown (a community for competitively-minded Pokemon battling as a strategy game) called Azure desperately needed an interface remodeling. This was for reasons of readability, aesthetics, and ease of use. They enlisted my help to remake their CSS for this purpose. The catch? Esoteric rules in their license agreement with the larger Showdown community allow them only to modify server-side code. They must use the same client as the rest of Pokemon Showdown, verbatim. My challenge is to redo the interface without modifying either the underlying JS code or the DOM. Spicy!
